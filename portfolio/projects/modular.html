Modular Dungeon Tiles

This system of creating modular terrain pieces for tabletop systems using 1" grids was a pet project of mine for a few months. The object files are currently under a non-commercial sharealike license while I figure out what i want to do with it next, but until then I'd encourage anybody with a 3d printer to try out the system!

The Base

The terrain base is where it all starts, with every other piece stemming off from it. With a triangular indent in the center, users can easily orient the bases in a grid with one another. The notch on the side also allows easy removal of the plates.

The Walls

The walls lock into a gap created by the grid of bases, allowing you to place four walls at one junction for maximum customizability in your tabletop

The Plate

This is where the system really shines. By allowing you to produce new tilesets at the blink of an eye with minimal material usage, you're able to create beautiful freeform landscapes without having to bust out the tape measures.

The 2x2 Plate

The two-by-two plate makes creating larger terrain features just that much simpler with a 2"x2" surface area. With the notches below lining up with the grid produced by the bases, you can easily fit larger display pieces into your custom dungeons
